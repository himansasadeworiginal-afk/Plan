tips_and_updates Proposal Rules

ALWAYS INCLUDE 3 TIERS

Makes the middle option the obvious choice. Most clients pick Business tier.

LEAD WITH THEIR PROBLEM

Don't start with your skills. Start with what they need fixed.

BE SPECIFIC ON DELIVERABLES

"5 pages" not "a website". Exact scope prevents scope creep later.

CLEAR CALL TO ACTION

End with a specific next step. "Reply to start" not "let me know".

gavel Contract Essentials

Every contract must include these 5 clauses to protect your time and money.

1. SCOPE (Exact Deliverables)

"Deliverables: [exact list]. Any work outside this scope requires a separate quote and will be billed at $XX/hr."

2. REVISIONS (Max 2 Rounds)

"Price includes 2 rounds of revisions. Additional revisions billed at $XX/hr. Revision requests must be submitted within 7 days of delivery."

3. PAYMENT (50% Upfront)

"50% deposit required before work begins. Remaining 50% due before final files are delivered. Late payments: project paused after 7 days."

4. KILL FEE (Cancellation)

"If client cancels after work begins, deposit is non-refundable. If client cancels after completion, 100% is due."

checklist Email Rules

REPLY WITHIN 24H

Fast replies win clients. Even if just to acknowledge.

UNDER 150 WORDS

Short emails get read. Long emails get ignored.

ALWAYS END WITH CTA

"Let me know by Friday" beats "let me know".

warning Handling Scope Creep

Firm but professional. Always redirect to the signed contract. Offer paid solutions.

RESPONSE: Small Addition Request

"Hi [Name], that's a great idea! That feature falls outside our agreed scope (per Section 1 of the contract). I can add it for $[Amount] with a [X day] delivery, or we can include it in a Phase 2. What would you prefer? Best, H. Sadew"

RESPONSE: "Just One More Thing"

"Hi [Name], I totally understand wanting to get this right. Per our agreement, we've completed [X deliverables]. Additional changes beyond the 2 included revision rounds are billed at $[Amount]/hr. I'm happy to do this — should I send a quick add-on invoice? Best, H. Sadew"

RESPONSE: Client Wants Everything for Free

"Hi [Name], I appreciate your enthusiasm for the project. To be transparent, the current request would add approximately [X hours] of work beyond our agreed scope. I want to deliver quality, so I have two options: (1) We stick to the original scope and deliver on time, or (2) We expand the scope with an adjusted timeline and budget. Which works best? Best, H. Sadew"

shield Scope Creep Prevention

ALWAYS HAVE A SIGNED CONTRACT

Verbal agreements don't protect you. Contract first, then work.

LIST EXCLUSIONS EXPLICITLY

"Not included: copywriting, photography, hosting setup, ongoing maintenance."

DOCUMENT EVERY CHANGE REQUEST

Email confirmation for every out-of-scope request. Creates paper trail.

NEVER SAY "YES" IMMEDIATELY

"Let me check the scope and get back to you" buys time to quote properly.

schedule 3-Message Follow-Up Sequence

No response? Send these in order. Never sound desperate.

DAY 1FRIENDLY

"Hi [Name], just sent over the proposal for [project]. Let me know if you have any questions — happy to jump on a quick call to discuss. Best, H. Sadew"

DAY 3VALUE REMINDER

"Hi [Name], wanted to follow up on the proposal. I also put together a quick [tip/idea/resource] that might help with [their problem] — [link]. Let me know if you'd like to move forward! Best, H. Sadew"

DAY 7FINAL

"Hi [Name], I know things get busy. I'm wrapping up [date] and wanted to check if you're still interested in moving forward. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all — just let me know so I can plan accordingly. Best, H. Sadew"

psychology Follow-Up Rules

NEVER SOUND DESPERATE

"No pressure" and "no worries" are your best friends.

ADD VALUE IN MESSAGE 2

Share a tip or resource. Shows you're helpful, not just chasing.

KNOW WHEN TO STOP

3 messages max. After that, they'll respond if interested.

"Hi [Name], thanks for sharing the details. The scope you've described would typically start at $[Amount] for the quality level needed. lightbulb Rejection Rules

NEVER EXPLAIN TOO MUCH

"Not the best fit" is enough. Over-explaining invites negotiation.

RECOMMEND SOMEONE ELSE

Shows goodwill. They'll remember you positively and may refer others.

LEAVE DOOR OPEN

"I'd love to work together in the future" — they might come back with budget.

DON'T APOLOGIZE

You're running a business. "I don't have capacity" is professional, not rude.

phone_in_talk Discovery Call Script

Opener → Their situation → Their problem → Desired outcome → Present solution → Price → Close. 15 minutes max.

THE SCRIPT (15 MIN):

MIN 0-2: OPENER

"Hi [Name], thanks for making time. Before we start — what made you reach out today?"

[Let them talk. Listen more than you speak.]

MIN 2-5: THEIR SITUATION

"Tell me about your business. Who are your customers?"

"What's working well right now?"

"What's frustrating you about your current website/presence?"

MIN 5-8: THEIR PROBLEM

"So if I understand correctly, the main issue is [repeat back their problem]?"

"How is this affecting your business? Lost leads? Lost sales?"

"What have you tried so far?"

MIN 8-10: DESIRED OUTCOME

"If we could fix this perfectly, what would that look like for you?"

"What does success look like 3 months after we launch?"

MIN 10-12: PRESENT SOLUTION
